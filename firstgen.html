First Generation Arab American Muslim

Essay By Judy Effendi • ~8 min read

On this page

Growing Up First Gen Questions & Comments Masking Identity Catholic HS Experience College & Culture Shock Owning Identity Now

Growing up First Gen

I grew up feeling confused: why don’t I get days off for Eid? Why does my mother wear a hijab? Why do my parents have an accent? Why am I being called a ‘terrorist’? Why do people make fun of the names of my family? Why am I looked at strangely for bringing grape leaves to lunch? Why am I not like my classmates? Why, why, why. Who was I, who did I want to become? I was torn between two different worlds, ideologies, morals, and cultures…

Growing Up First Gen

Growing up, I used to hate the side of me that was Middle Eastern and Muslim… Being first-gen is just like being Hannah Montana, living a double life where not many people really know both sides of you.

Questions & Comments I Heard

The list goes on… My response was always blank. I was embarrassed—of being different, of not celebrating Christmas, of not fitting in.

Masking Identity

I was surrounded by people in school who didn’t really know the real me, always masking my identity… I feared that if I embraced who I was, my peers would be embarrassed to be friends with me.

Catholic High School

Getting into high school, going to a Catholic school as an Arab-American Muslim was one hell of an experience… The beauty from that experience was meeting my best friend Ben—who was curious, supportive, and made space for all parts of me.

College & Culture Shock

College was the biggest culture shock… I lost myself trying to fit in. Eventually, I turned toward studies, service, and purpose—and found people who helped me love my cultural identity again.

Owning My Identity Now

As I navigate my life now, I’m more connected to my culture, faith, and self than ever before. I’m unapologetically me—proudly a First Generation Arab-American Muslim. If you read this, thank you for witnessing something vulnerable and true.
